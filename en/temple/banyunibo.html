BBanyunibo temple is a Buddhist temple built in the 9th century in the days of Ancient Mataram Kingdom. At the top of this temple there is a stupa which is characteristic of Buddhism. Banyunibo temples including Buddhist sacred buildings are quite rich in decoration (ornament). Almost in every part of the temple is filled by an assortment of ornaments and reliefs, although the one with the other decorative motifs that are often found together. The main temple facing west and lies between the sugar cane fields and rice fields. Of debris around, it is estimated there are 6 pieces perwara (accompanying temple) stupa around the main temple in the south and east. Now this is just an ancillary temples stupa base with rock debris scattered about. Perwara is not made ​​of andesite stone but that is easy to wear white.

Banyunibo Temple stands in between the vast rice fields and tucked in between the grove of trees and village roads were not paved smoothly. Entering the temple Banyunibo we will climb the ladder on the front right side and the left will be greeted lions. In the main room there is a temple Banyunibo eight windows, each of which is divided into two on each side of the temple, and three recesses without arcaberada right in the middle of the window. At the top of the outer temple Banyunibo, we will look at each of these niches which decorate the temple from all sides, and at the bottom of the temple we will find sculptures that surround this temple with some motifs.

In addition to describing the relief of Hariti who became a symbol of fertility goddesses in Buddhism, there is another picture where a woman surrounded by children. Vaisravana, husband Hariti is shown in a sitting position. Location Surrounded temple hill north, east and south. On the right wall there is relief "Goddess Hariti". Described children who are climbing up the tree and the Goddess Hariti that surrounded the children. Goddess Hariti in Buddhism is considered as a manifestation of the Goddess of Fertility, some are considered the Mother Goddess and Goddess of Wealth. This goddess is usually depicted as a goddess figure with prominent genetial tool and is always accompanied by his children.

Banyunibo Temple rarely visited by tourists. In addition because of its remote location, there is no public transport that specifically pass this temple, though its location is not difficult to reach, and can be reached by car about 25-30 minutes from Yogyakarta, or about 15 minutes from Prambanan Temple. From Yogyakarta up TransJogja 1A or 1B lane and stop at Prambanan shelter. Continue with ojek (motorcycle taxi) to the south until you see a road sign indicating the direction to the temple Banyunibo. Alternatively, from Prambanan we could also walk to get to the Temple Banyunibo. With private vehicles, departing from Yogyakarta to Solo Street or take the route to Prambanan. Arriving at the junction or market Prambanan Prambanan, turn right at the traffic lights. Continue straight for about 15 minutes until you find a road sign indicating the direction to the Temple Banyunibo. Follow the path until you find a sign of the temple.
