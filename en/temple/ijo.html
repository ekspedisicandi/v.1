Ijo temple is a Hindu temple complex which is expected to be built between the period of the 10th century until the 11th AD. This temple is a relic of the Kingdom Medang or so-called ancient Mataram or Hindu Mataram. Overall, the temple complex is a staircase terraces, with the bottom section on the west side and the highest being on the east, following the contours of the hill. Ijo Temple is located on the hillside and the cool hills. Visiting this temple we can find beautiful scenery that can not be found in other temples.

Ijo Temple is not accessible by public transportation. Travel to the Temple can be reached by following the green route to Prambanan using TransJogja bus lanes 1A or 1B and stop at Prambanan shelter. From Prambanan travelers heading south (to the right if departing from Yogyakarta, or to the left if from Solo) by using a motorcycle taxi service or taxi. Or by private car from the city center can be reached 27 minutes. After a journey of about 13 minutes, you will see the sign to Candi Ijo. Follow the sign to get Ijo temple complex located on the edge of the ramp Bukit Ijo. Residents ask for help if you get lost, or if using ojek motorcycle taxi be sure to wait because there is no public transportation there.
