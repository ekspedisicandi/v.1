Kalasan temple or Kalibening temple is the oldest Buddhist temple in Yogyakarta. This sacred building was erected to honor the Goddess Tara and a monastery for Buddhist monks. Rulers who govern the construction of this Kalasan named Maharaja Tejapurnapana Panangkaran (Rakai Panangkaran) of the family dynasty. Rakai Panangkaran is the king of the second Mataram Kingdom Hindu. This temple can be evidence of the presence of the Sailendra dynasty as rulers of Srivijaya in Sumatra over Java. In some of the temples Kalasan — except for the southern part of which is still intact, we would find it difficult to see its beauty because of the original stones are missing and some of the temples are only placed just tidaks esuai the usual position.

Kalasan is a blend of Buddhist and Hindu. The outside of the temple, there is a niche decorated with images god holds a lotus flower. At each entrance there is dijenggernya headdress when there are flower buds. There above the tree gods and celestial dwellers play sounds like a fiddle, drums, shells and pine. In the southern part of the temple there are two reliefs of bodhisattvas, while the roof is made up of 3 levels. In the southern part of the temple there is also a large Banaspati decorated with tendrils and makara-makara, which is including the results on the Hindu Javanese art. The roof is octagonal and two-story. On the first level there is a statue of Buddha surrounded by stupas.

In the period around the year 750 – 850 AD there are 2 major dynasty that controlled Central Java. In the north controlled by the dynasty or Hindu Sanjaya dynasty. While in the southern region controlled by the Buddhist Sailendra Dynasty. The two forces have made great dynasty temples that exist in both areas have 2 very different complexion. Finally, the two major dynasties were united by a marriage between Rakai Pikatan of Sanjaya dynasty and Pramodawardhani, son of Maharaja Samarattungga of Wangsa dynasty. The marriage occurred in about the year 838 – 851 AD. Kalasan built as a tribute to marriage Pancapana of the Sanjaya dynasty to Dyah Pramoedya Ward of the Sailendra dynasty.

Kalasan accessible by public transport. It is not too difficult to reach, and can be reached by car about 18 minutes from Yogyakarta, or about 4 minutes from Prambanan Temple. From Yogyakarta up TransJogja 1A or 1B lane and stop at Prambanan shelter. Continue with ojek (motorcycle taxi) to the temple. Alternatively, from Prambanan we could also walk to get to the Kalasan. With private vehicles, departing from Yogyakarta to Solo Street or take the route to Prambanan. From the direction of Yogyakarta, Prambanan Kalasan before.
